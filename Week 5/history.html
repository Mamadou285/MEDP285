→ The Photosynthesis Process

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants and some bacteria convert light energy, typically from sunlight, into chemical energy (sugars) using carbon dioxide and water, releasing oxygen as a byproduct.

→ How it Works?

  • Light Energy: Plants capture light energy using pigments like chlorophyll.
  • Reactants: They use carbon dioxide (CO2) and water (H2O).
  • Process: CO2 and H2O are converted into glucose and oxygen through chemical reactions.
  • Products: Glucose serves as food for the plant, and oxygen is released into the atmosphere.
